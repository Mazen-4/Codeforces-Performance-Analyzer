02 · Motivation

Practicing without a plan wastes time.

// 01

Drowning in problems

Codeforces hosts 9,000+ rated problems across 30+ tags. Without guidance, programmers grind random sets that don't move their rating.

// 02

Filters aren't strategy

Difficulty sliders pick problems near your rating, but say nothing about which topics are actually holding you back.

// 03

Invisible skill gaps

You don't know what similar-rated peers can solve that you can't. Self-assessment is unreliable and rarely tag-specific.

// 04

No personalized signal

"Top topics for 1600s" averages over thousands of people. Your weakness profile is yours — recommendations should be too.

05 · Feature Engineering

How we measure skill per topic.

tag_strength = 0.30 × acceptance_rate + 0.30 × difficulty_score + 0.20 × rating_boost + 0.10 × specialization + 0.075 × efficiency + 0.075 × volume
30%
Acceptance rate
Can you actually solve problems in this tag? Laplace-smoothed.
30%
Difficulty score
Average rating of solved problems in tag ÷ 3500.
20%
Rating boost
(current + peak rating) ÷ 7000 — overall standing.
10%
Specialization
How focused are you on this tag vs. all your solves?
7.5%
Efficiency
How often do you solve a problem on the first try?
7.5%
Volume
log(1 + solved) ÷ log(51) — diminishing returns.

07 · Models

Three LightGBM regressors, one ranking signal.

M1 · Success

Will you solve this problem?

solve_score = ever_AC − wa_count × 0.2
clipped to [−1, 1]
Sweet-spot filter: keep predictions in [−0.2, 0.8]. Challenging but reachable — the design decision that makes the rest work.
→ solved, 0 WA = +1.0
→ solved, 5 WA = 0.0
→ unsolved, 3 WA = −0.6
MAE0.26 R²0.27 AUC0.81 n335K
M2 · Attempts

How many tries before AC?

attempts = wa_count + 1
clipped to [1, 10]
Trained on solved problems only (~307K rows). Output bucketed for UI labels.
→ < 1.5 → easy
→ 1.5–2.5 → moderate
→ > 2.5 → hard
MAE0.88 targetsolved-only n307K

08 · Recommendation

From candidate pool to ranked list.

01
Build candidate pool: problems solved by neighbors, not by you — weighted by neighbor similarity.
02
Run success model → keep only solve_score ∈ [−0.2, 0.8] — the sweet spot.
03
Compute weakness_boost = mean((1 − user_strength[t]) × peer_strength[t]) over tags.
04
final_score = 0.6 × difficulty_match + 0.4 × weakness_boost
05
Sort by weakness_boost first, then final_score — skill gaps lead.

Which tag would buy the most rating?

1
For each of 20 tags, simulate +20% strength.

3
Count delta_problems entering the sweet spot.
4
estimated_rating_gain = (delta_problems / 10) × 50
5
Rank tags by estimated gain — that's your study order.

12 · Results & Takeaways

What we shipped, what we learned.

86%
weakness recall — KNN reliably surfaces peers who struggle with the same tags.
[−0.2, 0.8]
the sweet-spot filter on solve_score is the single most important design choice in the ranker.
5–10s
full pipeline latency from handle → ranked recommendations, on commodity hardware.
R² = −0.62
logistic regression collapses on skewed continuous targets — worse than predicting the mean.
→ counterfactual analysis turns a model output into an answer.
Instead of "here are 20 problems," the user gets "practice dp — it's worth +62 rating." The whole pipeline exists to make that one sentence trustworthy.
